# The Desk

### A Trading Partner for the Discretionary Trader

---

## The Problem

Every day, thousands of traders sit down alone — in a bedroom, a home office, a coffee shop — and go to war with the markets. They've done the work. They've studied price action, built strategies, backtested ideas, watched hundreds of hours of tape. They know what they're supposed to do.

And then the bell rings, and they don't do it.

They chase. They hesitate. They move their stop. They take a B- setup because they're bored. They skip the A+ setup because the last one like it stopped them out. They overtrade on Fridays. They revenge trade after a loss. They know all of this about themselves — and they do it anyway.

This isn't a knowledge problem. It's an execution problem. And it's the single biggest reason discretionary traders fail.

The tools that exist today don't solve this. Trading journals help you reflect *after* the damage is done. AI signal generators tell you what *they* think you should trade — not what *you* decided you should trade. Psychology coaches talk about mindset in the abstract without any connection to your actual strategy or live market conditions. Charting platforms give you data but no guidance. Prop firm dashboards track your metrics but don't help you improve them.

No one has built the thing that traders actually need: **a disciplined partner who knows your plan, watches the market alongside you, and helps you execute your own process in real time.**

Until now.

---

## What Is The Desk?

The Desk is a local-first trading suite and AI-powered coaching environment built for discretionary traders. It is not an auto-trader. It does not place orders. It does not generate signals from its own models. It does not tell you what to trade.

Instead, it does something far more valuable: **it helps you trade the way you already decided to trade.**

The Desk is built around a simple but powerful idea — that every trader already has a process, and the hardest part of trading isn't finding edge, it's *consistently executing* the edge you've already found.

When you sit down at The Desk, someone is already there. They've reviewed the overnight session. They know your playbook. They've identified which of your setups are most likely to trigger today. They've pulled your journal notes from the last time this market context appeared. They're ready to work.

Throughout your session, The Desk watches alongside you. It matches live market conditions against your predefined rules. It surfaces contextual prompts — not "BUY NOW," but "This looks like your VWAP pullback setup. Your backtest shows 67% win rate with 2.1R. Your rules say entry at 21,435 with a stop 8 points below." When you're in a trade, it tracks your targets and reminds you of your management plan. When you're about to deviate, it speaks up.

After the session, it helps you review. What did you take? What did you miss? Where did you follow the plan, and where did you stray? Over weeks and months, it identifies the patterns you can't see yourself — the behavioral tendencies that are quietly helping or hurting your performance.

**The Desk is your second seat. Your spotter. Your floor presence. The trading partner every solo trader wishes they had.**

## The Core Philosophy

The Desk is built on three beliefs:

**1. Your rules, not ours.**

Every trader is different. Scalpers, swing traders, order flow traders, price action traders, arbitrage traders — they all have different setups, different timeframes, different risk tolerances. The Desk doesn't impose a methodology. It takes *your* backtested strategies, *your* rules, *your* playbook, and turns them into a living system that works for you. The value isn't in the intelligence — it's in the discipline.

**2. Coaching, not automation.**

The Desk will never push a button for you. That's intentional. The moment a system starts executing trades, it becomes something you watch instead of something you use. The Desk keeps you in the driver's seat. It makes you a better trader — it doesn't replace you as a trader.

**3. The whole lifecycle, not just the session.**

Trading isn't just the hours between the open and the close. It's the research that sparks an idea. The discipline of formalizing that idea into testable rules. The rigor of backtesting it. The preparation of planning your session. The focus of executing in real time. The honesty of reviewing what happened. And the wisdom of recognizing patterns over time and evolving your approach. The Desk supports all of it, because improving at trading means improving the *entire process*, not just one piece.

## The Process Loop

The Desk is built around a seven-stage process that reflects how great traders actually develop and refine their craft:

### 1. Discover

Every strategy starts as a spark. You notice something — a recurring pattern on the chart, an insight from another trader's post, a behavior in the order flow that keeps appearing before a move. The Desk provides a research library where you capture these raw inputs: saved posts, links, screenshots, voice notes, freeform ideas. Everything is tagged and searchable so nothing gets lost.

*The Desk's role: Librarian. Organizes your research, connects related ideas, and surfaces past observations when relevant new data appears.*

### 2. Define

A vague idea isn't tradeable. The Define stage is where you formalize an observation into a structured, testable rule. The Desk provides a playbook builder — a guided framework that pushes you to specify the setup conditions, the entry trigger, the stop placement logic, the target(s), the position sizing rules, the market context where it applies, and the invalidation criteria.

This is one of the most valuable things The Desk does. Most traders skip this step or do it loosely in their heads. Then they wonder why they can't execute consistently — they never actually wrote down what the trade *is*.

*The Desk's role: Sparring partner. Asks the hard questions. "What confirms this entry? Where exactly is your stop? What context does this need? What invalidates the setup before entry?"*

### 3. Prove

Defined rules get backtested against historical data. The Desk provides tools to test your strategies and produce quantified results: win rate, average R-multiple, maximum drawdown, profit factor, performance by market condition, time of day, day of week, and more. These results get attached to each playbook setup, so when the setup triggers in real time, you know exactly what the historical edge looks like.

*The Desk's role: Analyst. Dispassionate, evidence-based. "This setup has a 62% win rate over 340 samples with an average R of 1.8. It underperforms on FOMC days."*

### 4. Plan

Before every session, The Desk generates a pre-session briefing based on current market context and your playbook. It identifies which of your setups are most likely to present today given overnight action, key levels, scheduled news, and current market regime. It reminds you of relevant journal notes. It sets the framework for what you're looking for and — just as importantly — what you're ignoring.

This is where discipline starts: before a single tick prints.

*The Desk's role: Briefing officer. "Overnight range is tight. Two of your setups are in play: the opening drive continuation and the failed breakdown. You noted last Thursday that you tend to force trades in low-range environments — consider a reduced size day."*

### 5. Execute

The live session. The Desk connects to real-time market data via DTC protocol, webhooks, or similar live feeds, and continuously evaluates current conditions against your playbook and your session plan. When conditions match a setup, it surfaces a contextual prompt with all the relevant data: the setup name, the entry price your rules specify, the stop, the targets, and the historical performance metrics.

When you're in a trade, The Desk tracks your position against your management plan. First target hit? "Your rules say trim half here." Price stalling at your second target with declining momentum? "This is where your plan says to consider closing the runner." About to take a third trade after two consecutive losses? "You set a max daily loss of 3R. You're at 2R drawdown. Your rule says one more full loss and you're done for the day."

It does not place trades. It does not manage orders. It coaches, prompts, and holds you accountable — in real time, with full context.

*The Desk's role: Spotter. Calm, precise, present. Watching everything, saying only what matters.*

### 6. Reflect

After the session, The Desk helps you review. Trade data can be auto-imported from your execution platform. Each trade is matched against your playbook: was it a planned setup? Did you follow your rules? Where did you deviate? The Desk provides a structured journaling framework — not just "what happened" but "why," with tags for emotional state, plan adherence, and setup quality.

Over time, this builds a rich, searchable archive of your trading history — not just P&L, but the *decisions* and *thought process* behind every trade.

*The Desk's role: Coach. Honest, constructive, specific. "You followed the plan on 4 of 6 trades. The two deviations were both impulse entries in the first 15 minutes. Your journal from last week shows the same pattern."*

### 7. Evolve

This is the long game. The Desk's pattern recognition engine looks across weeks and months of structured data to identify behavioral tendencies, performance trends, and strategic insights that you can't see in a daily review. Are your Wednesday results consistently better than Monday? Does your win rate drop after a big green day? Is one setup slowly decaying in effectiveness while another is improving?

These insights feed back to the top of the loop — generating new research questions, new ideas to define and test, and refinements to existing strategies.

*The Desk's role: Strategist. Big-picture, patient, data-driven. "Over the last 8 weeks, your VWAP pullback setup has produced 2.4R average on trend days but -0.3R on range days. Consider adding a trend filter to the setup definition."*

## The Architecture: Your Desk, Your Team

One of The Desk's most powerful features is its configurable AI architecture. Under the hood, The Desk uses specialized sub-agents — each responsible for a different piece of the analytical work:

- **Market Structure Agent** — monitors price action, key levels, and regime
- **Order Flow Agent** — watches volume, delta, and participation patterns  
- **Playbook Agent** — evaluates live conditions against your defined setups
- **Risk Agent** — tracks your open exposure, daily P&L, and rule compliance
- **Research Agent** — scans your library for relevant historical notes and context
- **Sentiment Agent** — monitors your trading behavior for signs of tilt, overtrading, or hesitation
- **News/Catalyst Agent** — tracks scheduled events and breaking developments

These sub-agents feed into a single orchestrator — **your named presence** — that synthesizes their outputs and decides what to surface, when, and how.

The presence is yours to configure. You choose the name. You set the personality — direct, conversational, minimal, analytical, motivational. You define behavioral rules: "Don't talk to me in the first five minutes." "Always remind me of max loss before my third trade." "If I haven't taken a trade in 90 minutes, check in." Over time, the presence learns from how you respond to its prompts and adapts accordingly.

Different traders will configure different agent weightings and different personalities. A scalper might lean heavily on the order flow and risk agents with a terse, fast-paced presence. A swing trader might emphasize the market structure and research agents with a calm, analytical voice. The underlying system is the same — the experience is uniquely yours.

**Every trader's Desk becomes their own.** Your playbook, your journal history, your patterns, your presence. That's not something you can screenshot and replicate. That's a trading practice built over months and years.

## What The Desk Is Not

- **It is not an auto-trader.** It will never place an order.
- **It is not a signal service.** It doesn't tell you what to trade — it helps you execute what *you* decided to trade.
- **It is not a black box.** Every prompt is traceable to a specific rule in your playbook, a specific data point from your backtest, or a specific note from your journal.
- **It is not a replacement for skill.** You still need to develop your own strategies, read the market, and make decisions. The Desk makes your existing skill more consistent — it doesn't substitute for it.
- **It is not cloud-dependent.** The Desk is local-first. Your data, your rules, your journal, your API keys — all on your machine. Privacy is not a feature, it's the architecture.

## The Vision

Trading is one of the few high-performance disciplines where practitioners routinely work alone, without support, in an environment specifically designed to exploit their psychological weaknesses. Surgeons have teams. Pilots have co-pilots. Athletes have coaches on the sideline during every game. Traders have a chair and a screen and their own thoughts.

The Desk changes that.

We believe that the gap between a trader's *knowledge* and their *execution* is the single largest source of lost edge in discretionary trading. The Desk exists to close that gap — not by adding more information, more indicators, or more complexity, but by helping traders do what they already know they should do.

**Backtest. Plan. Execute. Reflect. Evolve. Repeat.**

The Desk is where serious traders do serious work.

## The Commercial Opportunity

The Desk addresses an underserved niche at the intersection of trading technology and performance coaching:

**What exists today:**
- Trading journals (TraderSync, Trademetria) — retrospective, not real-time
- AI signal generators (Trade Ideas, TrendSpider) — their signals, not your rules
- Psychology coaching tools (PushButton Trading) — no connection to live data or strategy
- General AI agents (OpenClaw + trading plugins) — no structured playbook, no discipline loop
- Prop firm dashboards (Topstep Coach T) — ecosystem-locked, evaluation-focused

**What no one has built:**
A system that takes your backtested rules, connects to live market data, and provides real-time contextual coaching during your session — with a full lifecycle from research through evolution.

**The addressable market** is every discretionary trader who has a process but struggles to follow it consistently — prop firm traders, independent futures traders, forex traders, crypto traders, equity day traders. The discipline problem is universal across markets and styles.

**The business model** is coaching, not signals — which sidesteps most regulatory concerns around investment advice. The Desk doesn't tell you what to trade. It helps you follow your own plan.

**The moat** is accumulated personalization. Every trader's Desk becomes more valuable over time as their playbook, journal, backtest history, and behavioral patterns accumulate. This creates natural retention and switching costs that no competitor can replicate by copying features.
